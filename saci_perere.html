Saci Pererê (sah-see peh-reh-reh)

Guardian of the Forest



The legend of Saci-pererê has existed since the end of colonial times and has its origins in the indigenous tribes of southern Brazil.

The term “Saci” comes from the Tupi term sa'si, which is related to a bird, which is known by the names “Saci”, “Matimpererê” or “Martim-pererê” (in Tupi: matintape're).

Initially, Saci was portrayed as a black and devilish character, who had two legs and a tail. From the African influence, he loses his leg fighting capoeira and acquires the habit of smoking the pito, that is, the pipe. Saci-pererê's little red hat, in turn, comes from folklore in northern Portugal. It was used by the legendary Troll, who possessed supernatural powers.

The legend is told in all Brazilian regions and, therefore, the story changes depending on the location. In some places, it has different names such as: Saci-Cererê, Matimpererê, Matita Perê, Saci-Saçurá and Saci-Trique.


Saci-pererê is a very mischievous character who enjoys playing pranks on animals and people. According to the stories, his main pranks are:
  • Braiding animals' tails at night
  • Hiding objects (such as seamstresses' thimbles)
  • Whistling shrill tunes to scare travelers
  • Swapping the salt container for the sugar container
  • Distract the cooks so they can burn the food.
He is the guardian of herbs and medicinal plants, which is why it acts mischievously to those who try to take plants without authorization.

How To Capture a Saci?

Legend guarantees that to capture the Saci-pererê, a person must throw a sieve into the whirlwinds. After capturing it, it is necessary to remove its red hood to trap it in a bottle.

Where Did Saci Come From?

It is believed that Saci was born from the bamboo shoot, remaining there until he was seven years old and, after this period, he lives for another seventy-seven years practicing his mischief between humans and animals. Finally, when it dies, Saci becomes a poisonous mushroom. A black and small creature that inhabits forests and is infamous for the tricks and pranks they play on others. The legend of Saci originates from the Southern region of Brazil, it's story was influenced by elements from African and indigenous culture. It gained traction with the Brazilian public via legendary children's book author Monteiro Lobato.
