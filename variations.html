Variations

The figure of Iara finds parallels in various cultures around the world, which have their own versions of seductive and dangerous aquatic beings. The comparison between these mythological figures highlights both the singularities of the Mother of Waters and the universal similarities in the ways in which human beings imagine and relate to the natural world.
Region Similarities with Iara
NorthStrongly linked to the Amazon rivers, the northern version of Iara is closer to indigenous origin, portraying her as a powerful guardian of the waters.
Northeast In the northeastern region, Iara's stories often acquire mystical and syncretistic elements, mixing indigenous, African and European beliefs.
Southeast In urban areas and on the coast of the Southeast, Iara appears in narratives that reflect contemporary concerns, such as river pollution and the loss of connection with nature.
These parallels reveal how different cultures use mythological figures to express the admiration, fear and curiosity that waters – with their secrets and dangers – inspire in human beings.
