MYTHOLOGICAL LITTLE MISCHIEFS

     All across the world, regions have tales and stories of their very own mythical little people wandering the area. Some invoke fear in children so that they don't wander where they aren't supposed to. Others depict them as kind and existing in their own space. The common denominator in these specific mytholoogical creatures is that they play games and are mischevious.
     If you hover on origin in the top right, there will be more details on each region. I have minor stories that you should take with a grain of salt.

MEXICAN MYTHOLOGY  ->  DUENDES

Duendes are mischievous creatures from Mexican mythology, often depicted as small, childlike beings with pointed ears and a penchant for playing pranks on humans. Stories of duendes are told all over Latin America and is not unique to Mexican folklore, but these are stories I heard growing up from my parents.

CHEROKEE MYTHOLOGY  ->  LITTLE PEOPLE

In Cherokee mythology, Little People are a race of diminutive beings who live in the hills and mountains of Appalachia. They are believed to be the spirits of nature, closely connected to the land and its creatures. Other Native American tribes have their own depictions and recountings of little people.

MAINSTREAM MYTHOLOGY  ->  GOBLINS, FAIRIES, AND LEPRECHAUNS

Goblins, leprechauns, and fairies are iconic figures in mainstream mythology, often associated with European folklore. These beings are often said to inhabit natural places, such as forests, rivers, and hills, and they are closely tied to the cycles and spirits of nature. Each of these mythical creatures has its own unique characteristics and plays a role in folklore and storytelling.
